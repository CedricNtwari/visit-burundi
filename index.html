Discover Burundi


The Heart of Africa

About Burundi

Burundi is a landlocked country in the Great Lakes region of Africa and stretches over 27,834km2. Known as the heart of Africa, Burundi is famous for its rich and varied natural landscape characterized by picturesque lakes, rivers, reserves, grasslands, national parks and mountainous terrains.

As a relatively untouched destination on the African continent, Burundi's natural beauty offers a unique and authentic perspective and experience to visitors.

Facts Burundi

Where We Are

Burundi is located in east-central Africa. It's one of the smallest African nations at 27,834 square kilometers (10,747 sq mi).

Getting Here

Visitors to Burundi must get a visa before travel at arrival or via any Burundian diplomatic mission.

The Weather

Burundi has an extremely pleasant climate, temperatures remaining consistently warm in the day and cool at night year-round. Best time to visit is November to April. Check the Weather Forecast here.

Experience Burundi

Visiting Burundi is one of the best ways to support communities, wildlife conservation, and the long-term success of Africa's wild areas. Choose from the different accommodation options available below and enjoy a spectacular wildlife experience.

Burundi is a convenient and beautiful destination to explore. It can be a short getaway for those living in the region, a fascinating combination with nearby attractions, or an easy add-on before or after embarking on other adventures.

Karera Waterfalls

The falls of the Karera River are located in Rutana province, in the municipality of Musongati, 3 km away from the capital area of the Shanga zone. They are in a protected area covering 142 ha. There are four falls in the Kagera River, swelled by a branch from the Mwaro River coming in from the west which many believe is the source of the falls.
